UX(user experience)

Knowing what attracts your website visitors to your website is just as important as knowing the things about the same site that may be turning them off. This is a concept that most large firms and companies have known for a long time and having this in their armoury has proved a lethal weapon to wield in the battle for online customers and market share.

Simply put, the better and easier you make your website to navigate , the more enjoyable it becomes for the end user and therefore the longer people are willing to tolerate whatever it is that you are trying to sell because they don’t have the added burden of tolerating a tedious and unthoughtful customer experience.

From simple wording of statements, to positioning of buttons on a page, a lacklustre focus on user experience issues can cause great angst while a well thought out strategy could prove to be just right if you know what is needed to make a great website work.

Making a website is like making a great dish, adding too much seasoning could prove costly while too little could be seen as boring, lacking inspiration and mundein. You need a website which if you take time and invest a little bit of effort, turns out just right.
